R.E.D

Radiation Evacuation Device

when first starting this project we were thinking about how crazy it was that the threat of nuclear warfare is thrown around so easily between world leaders these days. We started thinking about the implications of nuclear warfare and the bombs that were dropped on Fukushima and Nagasaki, Japan. The people of Japan never quite recovered.

After the nuclear bombs were dropped a discriminative term was born. “Hibakusha” which means “explosion effected people”. The victims of nuclear radiation often had bad burns or scarring, which people thought was contagious or a genetic mutation. Those labelled Hibakusha are outcasted from society and it is often very hard for them to live normal lives when it comes to things like jobs and relationships. The term Hibakusha has been revived in Japanese culture due to the 2011 Fukushima-Daiichi nuclear disaster and people are facing the same discrimination once again.

 

We initially wanted to help these people faced with discrimination every day due to a severe lack of education and understanding. The driving factor behind discrimination is misinformation so we decided to raise awareness on nuclear energy and emergency preparedness by speculating a future where the government has put these kits in place to make sure the people know exactly what to do if an event like that were to occur again.

We decided that the best way to raise awareness and preparedness would be to implement a standardized kit for all residents. The kit would be installed into the home where all members of the household would be instructed of its use. The kit’s compartments break down into 3 different zones, based off of the amount of radiation you’re recieving. The amount of radiation you are receiving is monitored by a personal detection device. We have speculated that in our future every resident will have a personal detection device integrated into an everyday device or piece of clothing. When radiation levels become too high your detection device will alert you and your kit, which will unlock the corresponding compartments you need to survive to evacuation.

Zone 1 is yellow. The event is pretty insignificant and you are only exposed to slightly higher radiation levels than normal. This compartment contains a mask and suit to protect from harmful alpha and beta radiation particles.

Zone 2 is orange. A Zone 2 event is mildly destructive and you are exposed to high amounts of radiation that could cause radiation sickness within 3 days. This compartment contains a multi-tool, bleach to purify drinking water, and iodine tablets to prevent the development of thyroid cancer.

Zone 3 is red. If you survive an event of this magnitude you have been exposed to fatal levels of radiation.

 

One of the most challenging aspects came to us when we determined the mortality rates of the zones. In zone 3 you are exposed to fatal amounts of radiation and the only solution is death. But how do we make it ethical? How do we comfort people who are about to commit suicide? We decided that the products for zone 3 are cyanide infused to give people one last wordly comfort before they pass on.

Cyanide infused gummy bears for children or those not fond of needles.

Not everyone finds comfort in the same thing, so we came up with a variety of cyanide infused products to ease the victims into the reality of taking your own life instead of suffering from radiation sickness.
